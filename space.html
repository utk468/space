Space is a three-dimensional continuum containing positions and directions.
[1] In classical physics, physical space is often conceived in three linear dimensions.
Modern physicists usually consider it, with time, to be part of a boundless four-dimensional continuum known as spacetime.[2]
The concept of space is considered to be of fundamental importance to an understanding of the physical universe.
However, disagreement continues between philosophers over whether
it is itself an entity, a relationship between entities, or part of a conceptual framework.
